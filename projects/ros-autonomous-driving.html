Overview

Built a modular ROS-based autonomy stack that completed the full obstacle-and-sign course in 87 seconds with zero collisions, the fastest of all teams. In collaboration with Shuyu vanKerkwijk

Highlights

  • Modular ROS 1 + Gazebo architecture with dedicated nodes for control, perception, sign reading, and telemetry.
  • Trained four section-specific CNN pilots (Road, Gravel, Ramp, Off-Road) on 100k+ joystick-labeled images collected via a custom ESP32/joystick data engine at 20 Hz.
  • Robust obstacle logic: motion-based pedestrian detection, rule-driven truck & “Baby Yoda” avoidance, pink-line section transitions.
  • TFLite sign-reader (HSV mask → SIFT homography → CNN) with ~0.0018 s inference and ≈99.9% accuracy over 50 runs.
  • Consensus voting across multiple frames eliminated single-frame errors for perfect sign decoding.
  • 20+ consecutive fault-free runs; CNN outputs wrapped with safety filters to prevent low-speed creeping.
