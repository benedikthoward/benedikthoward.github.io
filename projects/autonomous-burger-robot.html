Overview

I built a 22-degree-of-freedom robot that grills, assembles, and plates plastic hamburgers autonomously. The system integrates custom electronics, real-time firmware, and a compact drivetrain designed to operate under competition constraints. In collaboration with Shuyu vanKerkwijk

Highlights

  • Custom PCBs: dual-channel H-bridge motor driver, IR-beacon band-pass + Schmitt trigger, and a main motherboard (two ESP32s + one STM).
  • FreeRTOS firmware orchestrating the drivetrain and 40+ sensors/actuators, real-time IR detection.
  • WebSocket interface on ESP32-S3 for live debugging, data logging, and calibration.
  • Compact mecanum drivetrain and 16 N·m worm-gear box from 1 mm bent aluminium to pivot the cranes.
  • Embedded bare-metal CV on ESP32-CAM for bun/patty/toppings detection under changing lighting.
